Expense Tracker

What does it do?

The Expense Tracker is a Google Sheet that can track categories of expenses and incomes, collecting them into a view-by-month dashboard. We'll describe each sheet, its purpose, and functionality.

Tracker

This is the starting sheet, and what we call the dashboard. The top-left beige box is the year. This is a dropdown that will automatically fill with years that are available within the data. The top blue is the months of the year. This shows that each column underneath is an income/expense for that month. The top green section is income and the bottom yellow is expenses. Each month's income, expense, and balance is calculated, as well as year-end totals. At the bottom in purple is a pie graph of the selected month or all year. It will show percentages and how categories compare to each other.

Expense Lookup

This sheet creates stats for a given query of expenses. You can search your expense by the following:

  • Start Date
  • End Date
  • Category
  • Merchant
There are also handy date presets that you can copy into the query fields to the right of the queries. Both category and merchant automatically generate a dropdown based on your current categories and expense data. Leaving a query box blank will accept expenses from all of the given query.

After entering the search criteria, you will see a list of expense underneath. These are the expenses that matched your criteria. The graph to the right shows the amount spend on a given day, as well as show a running total throughout the searched period.

Income Lookup

This sheet is identical to the Expense Lookup sheet, except that it searches through your income data instead of expenses.

Coupons/Gift Cards

This sheet is a handy place to remember any coupons and gift card balances. It even comes with highlighting the expiration date if the coupon is close to expiration.

Expenses

This sheet is where your expense data will live. This data is what is used in other sheets to aggregate and display. We'll talk about how to enter data below.

Income

This sheet is the same as the Expense sheet, except it stores income data.

API

This sheet contains handy information about the daily expense and income for each category. Each category has a named range, so other spreadsheets that you own can reference this data much easier.

FAQ

How Do I Input Data?

The Expense and Income sheets are set up that way because they are filled in by using a Google Form. There is a form for expenses and another for income. Generally don't try inputting data directly into these sheets, let the form do it. Occasionally, if you enter data out of chronological order, just sort the date column A-Z. Having data out of order can have unexpected results on the Lookup sheets. These forms come with the Expense Tracker and should show up in your Google Drive when you copy the template.

How Do I Copy This Tracker?

There is a link at the top of this page. If you are logged into your Google account, you can make a copy of the template by clicking "Copy Template" in the upper-right. This will automatically attach the forms as well. You can find these forms in your Google Drive.

Don't other services do this already, and automatically pull data?

Yes, there exist platforms that lets you link to your bank account and cards to automatically collect your expenses. I found these platforms to be kind of clunky, and won't let me adjust any of the data to my liking. For example, if you spent $150 at Target, some of it on groceries, some on clothes, and then some on a birthday gift, that might go in as a $150 grocery item. However, you can manually separate that out into the correct categories to get a more accurate view of your spending.

The forms should make this process easier. I find that adding a link to my phone's homescreen to the forms makes it easy to access and submit.

Does this collect my data?

All the data that is submitted in the forms and that exist on the spreadsheet does not leave the spreadsheet. I do not collect your data and I will not have access to any copies you make.

When I open the form, it's asking me to edit. How do I submit data?

Click the eye icon in the upper-right, with the tooltip "Preview" to open the actual form.

How do I change the categories?

There are a few places you'd have to touch to add/remove a category:

  1. Add the category below an already existing one. If there's no room for another one, then create a new row and copy the functionality from the row above it.
  2. Open up "Named Ranges" and look for either "Expenses" or "Income". Make sure these ranges are updated to include the new category.
  3. Update the form to include the new category in its dropdown.
  4. Expenses are color coded. To add a new color, look at the conditional formatting rules for each sheet that has colored category names and copy their behavior.

What if I screw something up? Do I lose all my data?

I recommend you archive a copy of the tracker every month into another folder, just in case. If your data is fine, but something else messed up beyond repair, you can always copy the template again and then copy your data directly into the form sheets. The form is usually smart enough to just add new data at the next blank line in the sheet.
